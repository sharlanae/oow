Help Save The Elephants

From the years 2010 to 2012, an estimated 100,000 elephants were killed across Africa. At this rate of decline, African elephants could be extinct in less than 10 years. Many other species rely on elephant dung, and its contents, as a food source. As a result, if elephants go extinct, other species could potentially be gone forever as well, but you can help prevent this. For more information, please click the "More Info" tab on the navigation bar.

African Elephants

African Savannah Elephants weigh up to 14,000 pounds and can grow up to 13 feet tall.

Elephant Tusks

Many African elephants are hunted because of their ivory tusks.

Baby Elephants

Baby elephants are born standing approximately 3 feet tall and weighing about 200 pounds.
